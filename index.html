    How to play

    1. Sixteen pieces lie strewn across the desk. Drag them around to spread out and read them.
    2. Slides and X-ray films are unreadable on their own. Put a slide on the microscope stage, or lay a film over the light box, to see what's on it.
    3. Drag four pieces that share something into one tray, then press Lock In.
    4. Right, and the tray locks with the group's name. Wrong, and you spend one of four guesses. "One away!" means three of the four belonged together.
    5. Stuck on a piece? The label printer will print its name on it. You get three labels per puzzle.

    Settings

    Theme, sound, and Casual mode (unlimited mistakes and labels) live in the settings panel.

    How to play

    1. Sixteen pieces lie strewn across the desk. Drag them around freely to spread out and read them.
    2. Sticky notes, papers, photographs, and prescription scripts read directly. Slides don't: put one on the microscope stage (each carries a letter, A, B, C, so you can tell them apart) and it appears on the big viewer. Drag the zoom between 4×, 10×, and 40×, and pan around.
    3. X-ray films only reveal themselves over the light box: whatever part of the film lies on the glass lights up. Slide one across to read it.
    4. Drag four pieces that share something into one tray, then press Lock In. Wrong costs one of four guesses. "One away!" means three of the four belonged together.
    5. Stuck? Drop any piece on the label printer and it prints the piece's name on a small label. Three per puzzle, unlimited in Casual mode.
    6. Some puzzles use fewer machines; the desk only shows what that puzzle needs.
    7. Keyboard: Tab to a piece, 1–4 sends it to a tray, 0 or Backspace returns it to the desk, V views a slide or film on its machine, L prints a label, arrows nudge.
